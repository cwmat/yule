A Feast of Yule

Hearken, friends, to winter's deep call,
To a feast where night drapes its shadowy shawl.
In the heart of the forest where the cold winds blow,
We summon the spirits of yule's fire glow.

Come forth to the circle, the sacred ring,
Where the dancers leap and the druids sing.
With holly adorned and the mistletoe near,
We'll honor the Sun's rebirth, the Wheel of the Year.

Let the Yule log burn and the old year die,
And the stars in the winter sky stand high.
Rejoice in the darkness, embrace the night,
For the dawn of the Solstice brings promise of light.

Raise up your chalice, your voice, and your cheer,
For the magic of Yule is undeniably here.
In the frosted embrace of winter's might,
We'll find warmth in the earth's slow tilt towards light.

By moonlight, we gather, we merry few,
To feast and to revel in frost's crisp hue.
With hearts open wide and spirits free,
We'll chant the old chants and be what we'll be.

So come, all ye pagans, children of the craft,
With nature's raw beauty, let our spells be cast.
Together we'll stand as the old year departs,
To welcome the new with hopeful hearts.
